AIvanceWorks has no real clients or completed engagements yet. Every stat, percentage, metric, case study, testimonial, or "clients see" claim on the website is by definition NOT from real past work.

**Why:** The company is in its go-to-market build phase. All website content is being authored before revenue exists. This means the constitution's content integrity rules (`no fabricated statistics`, `no invented case studies`, `no unauthorized logos`) are especially load-bearing — there are zero verified outcomes to draw from, only industry research, capability claims, and target/projected numbers.

**How to apply:**

1. **Every numeric claim must be one of three things:**
   - An industry range with a real, citable source (e.g., "HIMSS 2024 benchmark")
   - A capability claim, not an outcome claim ("our architecture is built to deliver sub-1.5s LCP" — not "our clients see sub-1.5s LCP")
   - Removed entirely

   Never "we achieved X for our clients" or "on average we deliver Y."

2. **`CaseStudySpotlight`, `TestimonialStrip`, and `LogoWall` sections are omitted from every page** until real, written-permission case/testimonial/logo content exists. A "we'd love to be your first" framing is acceptable where a spotlight would otherwise sit.

3. **Aggregate stats are banned.** "$2.3M saved," "300% ROI on average," "90% client satisfaction" — all forbidden, because we cannot back them with data.

4. **Process durations, pricing, and engagement model copy are "planned" or "typical" ranges**, not "our average from past projects." Language like "we typically deliver" is preferable to "we delivered."

5. **The `_unverified` list on every data file will be long.** In greenfield, virtually every marketing-normal claim needs a flag because it cannot be backed by client data. The `_unverified` list becomes the working to-do for the content/sales team to eventually populate with citations, replace with capability framing, or remove before launch.

6. **Signature sections should favor capability showcases and architecture/process diagrams over transformation narratives.** Before/after comparisons, "clients saw X% uplift" stories, and conversion-data visualizations all require real outcome data. Bento capability grids, architecture maps, persona comparisons, and methodology diagrams do not. For greenfield, the latter family is the safer signature choice.

7. **When migrating existing marketing copy** (e.g., the legacy `src/data/solutions.ts` content), assume every stat was aspirational and needs the same three-option treatment above. Do not port client-outcome framings into the new data files.
