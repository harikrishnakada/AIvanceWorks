Signature · Product Discovery
What you walk in with vs. what you walk out with
Before / After transformation. The fastest way to sell a discovery sprint is to show the concrete artifacts you hand over at the end.
Before Discovery
A vague idea with unknown risks
  • "We want to build an AI-powered tool"
  • No clarity on users, problem, or competitors
  • No validated scope or MVP definition
  • Unknown technical risks and unknowns
  • Uncertain investment and timeline
2-Week
Discovery
After Discovery
A prioritized, de-risked plan
  • Validated problem statement + target users
  • Prioritized MVP backlog with acceptance criteria
  • Technical architecture + tech-spike results
  • Risk register with mitigations
  • Fixed-scope proposal with 90% confidence estimate
Why this section: Product Discovery buyers are asking "what do I actually get?" The Before/After shows the tangible deliverables side-by-side, which is more convincing than a bulleted capability list.
Signature · MVP Development
From kickoff to real users in 12 weeks
Roadmap timeline. MVP buyers think in weeks and milestones — show them exactly what happens in each phase.
Week 1
Kickoff
Scope lock, sprint 0, CI/CD skeleton deployed
Weeks 2-4
Core Build
Auth, core data models, primary happy-path UI
Weeks 5-8
Real Users
Internal beta, first external users, weekly iteration
Weeks 9-11
Harden
Performance, observability, security review, billing
Week 12
V1 Launch
Production deploy, runbook, handover, metrics
Why this section: Founders/PMs buying MVP work are anxious about time-to-market. A 5-step roadmap with deliverables per phase reassures them that the schedule is real and thought through.
