Best for:

⚠️ Trade-off
Metrics card moves below the hero (into MetricsStrip). You lose the immediate "proof points in the hero" but gain visual warmth. Can also overlay small metrics on the image.

Best for:
Pages where the industry itself is the selling point — "we understand your world." High emotional impact. Works when you have a great photo. Risky with mediocre stock photos — can look generic fast.
⚠️ Trade-offs
• No room for metrics card in the hero
• Text readability depends on photo + scrim quality
• Needs a genuinely good photo — bad stock kills credibility
• Largest performance cost (LCP image)

Decision Framework: When to Use Which

Signal Text-Only Split Full-Bleed
Buyer wants proof first ✓ — —
Buyer wants to feel understood — ✓ ✓
Strong industry-specific photo available — ✓ ✓
Metrics are the hero's main job ✓ — —
Service is abstract / process-driven ✓ — —

💡 My Recommendation for Your 5 Pages

Solutions (industry pages) → Split hero. These pages sell domain expertise — showing the industry builds trust.
Services (process pages) → Text-only hero. Product Discovery and MVP Dev are abstract processes — a stock photo of "people at whiteboards" adds nothing the headline doesn't already say.

This gives you a natural visual distinction between solution and service pages without breaking the shared design system.
